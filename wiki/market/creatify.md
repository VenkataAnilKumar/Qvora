# Creatify — Competitor Deep-Dive

## TL;DR
Creatify is the primary competitor and strongest market validator — $650M+ ad spend, 15K+ brands, $24M raised. Their core weakness is no strategy layer before generation: they're template-first. Qvora's wedge is exactly what Creatify lacks.

## Workflow (4 Stages)

1. **Discover** — Browse winning ad templates by industry & platform; analyze competitor creative strategy; remix proven concepts
2. **Create** — URL-to-video, image-to-video, or scratch; UGC or cinematic style; batch create up to 50 variants at once
3. **Launch** — Connect ad accounts (Meta, TikTok, AppLovin); auto-optimized specs; built-in A/B testing
4. **Optimize** — Track spend, ROAS, CTR per creative; compare variants; identify winning hooks and angles

## What Creatify Does Well

- **Scale and trust** — proof point: 15K brands, $650M managed, major press
- **Ad account integration** — Meta, TikTok, AppLovin connected for direct launch and analytics
- **Template library** — browse winners by industry, remix instantly
- **Batch creation** — 50 variants at once
- **Analytics** — tracks ROAS, CTR, spend per creative; identifies winning hooks

---

## What Creatify Does NOT Do

- ❌ **No creative strategy layer** — starts with templates, not with "what angle should we use and why"
- ❌ **No brief engine** — no GPT-powered angle generation before the video
- ❌ **No learning closed loop** — analytics are manual; no automatic "next generation" from performance data
- ❌ **No hook/angle persistence** — each generation is largely independent

---

## Implication for Qvora

Creatify validates the market ($650M, $24M raised) and proves agencies pay for this category. Qvora's differentiation lives entirely in what Creatify lacks:

> The brief engine and the learning loop. Start smarter (strategy → video) and compound over time (performance → better brief → better video).
